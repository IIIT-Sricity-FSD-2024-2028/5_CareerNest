How CareerNest Works

A streamlined four-step journey from opportunity submission to successful placement.

01
✈️

Opportunity Submission

Recruiters submit job and internship opportunities with defined eligibility criteria. Placement officers review and approve them for publication.

🤝

Frequently Asked
Questions

CareerNest is designed for four key stakeholders in the campus placement ecosystem: Candidates (students seeking jobs/internships), Recruiters (companies hiring through campus), Alumni (graduates who can provide referrals), and Senior Placement Officers (who manage and oversee the process).

When recruiters post opportunities, they define eligibility criteria such as minimum CGPA, branch, graduation year, and other requirements. The system automatically validates each candidate's profile against these criteria and only allows eligible students to apply, eliminating manual eligibility checking errors.

Students can request referrals from alumni who are currently working in companies they've applied to. Alumni receive the request, review the candidate's profile and resume, and can choose to approve or decline. Approved referrals appear as indicators on the candidate's application, boosting their visibility to recruiters.

Yes. When reviewing candidate applications, recruiters can see referral indicators showing whether a candidate has been endorsed by an alumnus. This adds a layer of credibility and helps recruiters identify strong candidates who come recommended by professionals within their organization.

The Senior Placement Officer acts as the gatekeeper of the placement process. They review and approve job opportunities before they're published to students, enforce institutional placement rules and policies, monitor overall recruitment activity, manage recruiter relationships, and access comprehensive placement statistics and reports.
